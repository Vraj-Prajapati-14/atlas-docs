# 1. Purpose

This document introduces the restaurant industry from a business perspective.

The objective is **not** to study software.

The objective is to understand the business ecosystem in which our product will operate.

Every future engineering decision—including database design, APIs, AI capabilities, workflows, user roles, and infrastructure—must align with the operational realities documented here.

---

# 2. Definition

A restaurant is a commercial establishment that prepares, serves, and sells food and beverages to customers for immediate consumption, takeaway, delivery, or catering.

Modern restaurants are no longer only food businesses.

They are operational businesses involving:

* Inventory Management
* Procurement
* Kitchen Operations
* Customer Service
* Finance
* Marketing
* Human Resources
* Compliance
* Technology
* Delivery Platforms
* Analytics

Project Atlas aims to digitize and optimize this complete ecosystem.

# 5. Primary Business Objectives

Although every restaurant is unique, most businesses pursue the following objectives:

1. Increase revenue.
2. Improve profitability.
3. Reduce food wastage.
4. Improve customer satisfaction.
5. Increase repeat customers.
6. Optimize inventory.
7. Improve employee productivity.
8. Maintain regulatory compliance.
9. Expand to multiple locations.
10. Build a recognizable brand.

Project Atlas should support these objectives directly or indirectly.

---

# 6. Revenue Sources

Restaurants may generate income through multiple channels.

| Revenue Stream      | Description                                   |
| ------------------- | --------------------------------------------- |
| Dine-in             | Customers eat at the restaurant               |
| Takeaway            | Customers collect food                        |
| Home Delivery       | Restaurant-managed delivery                   |
| Aggregator Delivery | Orders via platforms such as Zomato or Swiggy |
| Catering            | Events and corporate functions                |
| Corporate Contracts | Recurring business agreements                 |
| Subscription Meals  | Weekly or monthly meal plans                  |
| Merchandise         | Branded products                              |
| Gift Cards          | Prepaid restaurant vouchers                   |
| Cooking Classes     | Additional customer experiences               |

Modern restaurant software must support multiple revenue streams instead of assuming only dine-in billing.

# 7. Major Cost Centers

Understanding costs is critical because software should help control them.

| Cost Category | Examples                          |
| ------------- | --------------------------------- |
| Food Cost     | Ingredients, beverages, packaging |
| Labor Cost    | Salaries, overtime, benefits      |
| Rent          | Property lease                    |
| Utilities     | Electricity, gas, internet        |
| Technology    | POS, software subscriptions       |
| Marketing     | Advertising, loyalty campaigns    |
| Delivery      | Third-party commissions           |
| Maintenance   | Equipment servicing               |
| Taxes         | GST and statutory obligations     |
| Miscellaneous | Cleaning supplies, uniforms       |

Reducing these costs without affecting service quality increases profitability.

# 9. Common Operational Challenges

The restaurant industry faces recurring operational problems.

### Financial

* Low profit margins
* Rising ingredient costs
* Cash leakage
* Discount abuse

### Operational

* Slow billing
* Long waiting times
* Inventory shortages
* Food wastage

### Human Resources

* High employee turnover
* Staff shortages
* Training inconsistencies
* Attendance management

### Customer Experience

* Delayed service
* Incorrect orders
* Poor complaint handling
* Low repeat visits

### Technology

* Multiple disconnected systems
* Manual reporting
* Duplicate data entry
* Lack of real-time visibility

These problems define the opportunity space for Project Atlas.

---

# 10. Project Atlas Perspective

Project Atlas should not merely digitize existing manual processes.

Its purpose is to improve business outcomes through:

* Automation
* Real-time visibility
* Predictive analytics
* AI-assisted decision making
* Standardized workflows
* Operational transparency

Success should be measured by measurable business improvements rather than feature count.

---

# Key Takeaways

* Restaurants are operational businesses, not just billing businesses.
* Multiple departments must work together efficiently.
* Software must support the complete business lifecycle.
* Business understanding must precede software design.
* Every future module should map back to one or more business objectives documented here.
