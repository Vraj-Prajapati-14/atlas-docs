# Purpose

This document serves as the entry point to the Restaurant Domain Knowledge Base.

Before designing software, every engineer, designer, product manager, business analyst, QA engineer, and AI engineer working on Project Atlas must understand how restaurants actually operate.

This document intentionally contains **business knowledge**, not implementation details.

No software architecture decisions should be made until the business domain is understood.

---

# Why This Document Exists

Many restaurant management systems fail because they are built by software engineers who understand technology but not restaurant operations.

Project Atlas follows the opposite philosophy.

We first understand:

* How restaurants earn money
* How restaurants lose money
* Daily operations
* Staff responsibilities
* Customer lifecycle
* Vendor lifecycle
* Inventory lifecycle
* Kitchen workflow
* Compliance
* Financial operations

Only after mastering these concepts will software requirements be defined.

---

# Scope

This knowledge base covers the complete restaurant business ecosystem.

Included topics:

* Restaurant Industry
* Restaurant Business Models
* Restaurant Types
* Restaurant Hierarchy
* Restaurant Roles
* Restaurant Departments
* Restaurant Operations
* Restaurant Workflows
* Restaurant Terminology
* Restaurant Financial Concepts
* Restaurant Documents
* Restaurant Hardware
* Restaurant Integrations
* Restaurant Compliance
* Restaurant KPIs
* Restaurant Pain Points
* AI Opportunities

Excluded topics:

* Software architecture
* APIs
* Database design
* UI/UX
* Programming
* Infrastructure

Those subjects are documented separately.

---

# Learning Objectives

After completing this domain knowledge, every team member should be able to:

* Explain how a restaurant operates from opening to closing.
* Identify every major stakeholder.
* Understand restaurant profitability.
* Understand inventory flow.
* Understand customer flow.
* Understand operational bottlenecks.
* Speak confidently with restaurant owners and managers.
* Translate business problems into software requirements.
